T.O.P.

Choi Seung-hyun (최승현), better known as T.O.P. is a South Korean rapper, actor, songwriter and singer and was born on November 4th, 1987. He is a member of the South Korean Boy Band Big Bang and is associated with the YG Entertainment music industry.

Prior to his involvement with his boy band and the YG Entertainment industry, T.O.P. was formerly an underground rapper under the name of Tempo and his childhood friend; also in the boy band, G-Dragon was also involved in this association. The leader of Big Bang, G-Dragon claimed that the two were "neighbourhood" friends from middle school and would often get together to dance and rap. Later on, G-Dragon has moved away and became distant with T.O.P. and soon after, was contacted again by his close friend to be scouted for a boy band associated with YG Entertainment, who asked him to audition. He was initially rejected due to a "chubby" appearance and didn't fit the idealistic version of an idol. For the next few months, he had worked on his physique, exercised and dieted to join YG Entertainment because it was in his intention to be in the music industry. Six months later, he returned for another audition and was signed on.

Choi Seung-hyun was given a stage name of T.O.P. by another artist named Se7en who was also associated with YG Entertainment. He became one of the two rappers within his group Big Bang, along with G-Dragon. The two were paired with four other people: Taeyang , Daesung, Seungri and So-1. Not too long later, So-1 was dropped by the record label and the 5-man South Korean boy band made its debut with five members.

After the band's albums and singles, T.O.P. began working a solo career. In April 2007, he featured with his crew memebers G-Dragon and Taeyang in singer Lexy's single "Super Fly" for her album Rush. He became the first member of Big Bang to become a star in acting, starring in a KBS2 Drama, I Am Sam, as the school's top fighter, Chae Musin. T.O.P. after a while put his solo career on hold as he joined Big Bang for their promotional activities. After a year or so of promoting the group's material, he returned to acting, playing the assassin Vick in the Korean TV drama Iris. In November 2010, YG Entertainment annouced T.O.P. and crewmate G-Dragon would release a collaboration album under the name GD&T.O.P. The duo held a world-wide showcase for their collaboration album at Times Square in Yeongdeungpo in Seoul, South Korea which also broadcasted live on YouTube.
